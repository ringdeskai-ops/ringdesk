Do I Need Planning Permission for Burglar Alarm Installation in Harrow?

Planning permission is not required for standard burglar alarm installation in Harrow, but external sounders must comply with noise regulations and be positioned considerately. Listed buildings in Harrow Conservation Area may require additional approvals for external equipment.

Under the Noise Act 1996, alarm sounders must not exceed 15 minutes duration and 120dB at one metre distance. In HA1 Harrow, many properties fall within conservation areas where external equipment positioning requires careful consideration of visual impact.

For Grade II listed properties common around Harrow School area, we recommend wireless systems to avoid drilling historic fabric. Our engineers work with conservation officers when necessary to ensure compliance while maintaining security effectiveness.

How Do I Choose Between Wired and Wireless Systems?

Choose wired systems for new builds and major renovations where cable runs are accessible, and wireless systems for existing properties, listed buildings, or when minimal disruption is essential. Wireless technology now offers equivalent reliability with easier expansion capabilities.

Wired systems like the Texecom Premier Elite provide unlimited power supply and interference-free communication. They're ideal when property access allows discreet cable routing through loft spaces or during refurbishment projects.

Modern wireless systems overcome traditional limitations through advanced encryption and long-life batteries. The Ajax system's Jeweller protocol provides 2,000m range with military-grade security, while 8-year detector batteries eliminate frequent maintenance.

Hybrid systems offer the best of both worlds, using wired connections where convenient and wireless for difficult areas. This approach is particularly effective in Harrow's mixed property types, from modern developments to period conversions.

What Maintenance Do Burglar Alarms Require?

Burglar alarms require annual professional servicing to maintain insurance validity and optimal performance, with basic user maintenance including monthly test activations. Professional burglar alarm service costs £89-£149 annually depending on system complexity.

Annual servicing includes battery replacement, detector cleaning, signal strength testing, and software updates. Our engineers also check for environmental damage and update user codes as required.

Between service visits, users should test systems monthly using designated test buttons, keep spare remote batteries available, and report any unusual behaviour immediately. Most modern systems provide low battery warnings 30 days before replacement needed.
