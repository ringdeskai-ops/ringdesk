Do I Need Planning Permission for Alarm Installation?

Planning permission is not required for intruder alarm installation on residential properties in London, but external sounders must comply with permitted development rights under the Town and Country Planning Act.

External alarm boxes must be positioned below the roofline and painted to match the building where possible. In conservation areas like parts of Harrow and Kingsbury, additional restrictions may apply regarding visual impact on streetscenes.

For Grade II listed buildings, Listed Building Consent may be required for external sounders or if cable routes affect historic fabric. Our surveys always include planning compliance checks as standard across all London service areas.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a basic intruder alarm cost to install?

A basic 4-zone intruder alarm costs £450-£750 to install in London, including PIR motion sensors, door contacts, control panel, and external sounder. Wireless systems cost £100-£200 more but install faster with less disruption.

Do intruder alarms reduce home insurance premiums?

Grade 2 certified intruder alarms typically reduce home insurance premiums by 5-15% annually. Systems must be professionally installed and annually maintained to qualify for insurance discounts with most providers.

Can I control my alarm remotely via smartphone?

Modern systems like Texecom Connect, Ajax Security System, and Pyronix HomeControl+ offer full smartphone control including arm/disarm, zone bypass, and real-time notifications. Remote control requires cellular or WiFi communication modules.

What happens if my alarm activates accidentally?

Accidental activations trigger a 30-second entry delay allowing disarming via keypad or smartphone app. If unsilenced, the external sounder activates for 15 minutes maximum as per BS EN 50131 requirements.

How long do wireless alarm sensors last?

Quality wireless sensors like Ajax devices offer 5-7 year battery life under normal use. The system alerts users via smartphone app when batteries reach 20% capacity, typically 6 months before replacement needed.
