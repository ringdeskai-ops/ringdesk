How Much Do Burglar Alarm Systems Cost in the UK?

Burglar alarm system costs in the UK start from £299 for basic wireless setups, with professional installation adding £150-£400 depending on complexity. Premium systems with smart features and professional monitoring reach £1,500+ for comprehensive coverage.

DIY wireless systems from Currys or Screwfix cost £99-£299 but lack professional-grade sensors and monitoring integration. Professional burglar alarm installation ensures BS EN 50131 compliance and police response eligibility.

Should I Choose Wireless or Hardwired Burglar Alarms?

Wireless burglar alarms are ideal for most modern homes due to faster installation, no structural damage, and advanced smart features. Hardwired systems suit new builds, commercial properties, or high-security applications requiring Grade 3 certification.

Wireless advantages include:

  • Installation in 1-2 days versus 3-5 days for hardwired
  • No cable runs through walls or ceilings
  • Easier relocation and expansion
  • Built-in anti-jamming and encryption
  • Smart app control and notifications

Hardwired systems offer:

  • No battery maintenance requirements
  • Higher security grades (up to Grade 4)
  • Immune to wireless interference
  • Lower long-term operating costs
  • Preferred by commercial insurers

Visonic PowerMaster systems bridge this gap with PowerG technology, offering wireless convenience with hardwired-level security and 8km professional range.

Do I Need Professional Monitoring for My Burglar Alarm?

Professional monitoring is essential for police response and insurance compliance in the UK. Only Gold or SSAIB approved alarm receiving centres can request police attendance, making self-monitoring insufficient for comprehensive security.

Monitored systems connect via dual-path communication (landline plus mobile/IP) to 24/7 control rooms. When triggered, operators verify the alarm within 60 seconds and dispatch keyholders or emergency services as required under BS 8418 standards.

Self-monitoring through smartphone apps provides immediate notifications but relies entirely on your response. This approach suits low-risk properties or budget-conscious users but won't satisfy insurance requirements or police response protocols.

Professional monitoring costs £15-£35 monthly but includes:

  • Police response authorisation
  • Keyholder management
  • Technical fault reporting
  • Insurance compliance certificates
  • System health monitoring

What Components Do I Need for a Complete Burglar Alarm System?

A complete burglar alarm system requires a control panel, PIR detectors, door/window contacts, external sounder, and user interface. Professional systems also include backup batteries, communicators, and tamper protection on all components.

Essential components include:

Control Panel: The system brain managing all sensors and communications. Texecom Premier Elite 24 supports 24 zones with built-in communicators, while Ajax Hub 2 Plus handles 150 wireless devices with LTE backup.

PIR Detectors: Motion sensors covering internal areas. Pet-immune models like Texecom Capture D20 ignore animals up to 20kg while detecting human movement patterns.

Door Contacts: Magnetic switches on entry points. Wireless models like Ajax DoorProtect Plus include tilt and shock detection for comprehensive door monitoring.

External Sounder: Visible deterrent with strobe light meeting 100dB minimum requirements under BS EN 50131. Texecom Odyssey X3 includes anti-foam protection and battery backup.

Keypads: User control interface for setting/unsetting. Modern systems use proximity tags or smartphone apps instead of traditional PIN codes.

Communicators: Dual-path communication devices ensuring monitoring centre connectivity via multiple networks for redundancy.

How Do I Upgrade My Existing Burglar Alarm System?

Upgrading existing burglar alarm systems involves compatibility assessment, component replacement, and professional reconfiguration to meet current standards. Alarm system upgrades typically focus on control panels, wireless sensors, and smart connectivity features.

Common upgrade scenarios include:

Panel Replacement: Older systems using discontinued parts benefit from modern panels maintaining existing wiring. Texecom Premier Elite panels accept most hardwired sensors while adding wireless expansion capability.

Wireless Migration: Converting hardwired sensors to wireless eliminates maintenance and adds smart features. Risco Agility systems excel at hybrid installations combining existing wiring with new wireless zones.

Smart Integration: Adding app control and notifications to traditional systems through compatible communicators or complete panel upgrades.

Systems over 10 years old typically require complete replacement due to obsolete technology and discontinued support. Professional assessment determines upgrade feasibility and cost-effectiveness versus new installation.

In areas like Northolt, Ilford, and Uxbridge, many 1990s-2010s installations use Scantronic or Honeywell panels requiring modernisation for continued insurance compliance and monitoring support.

What Maintenance Do Burglar Alarm Systems Require?

Burglar alarm systems require annual professional servicing plus regular user maintenance to ensure reliable operation and insurance compliance. Professional alarm servicing includes component testing, battery replacement, and certification documentation.

Annual maintenance tasks include:

  • Control panel diagnostics and firmware updates
  • Sensor testing and calibration
  • Battery voltage testing and replacement
  • Communication path verification
  • External sounder and strobe functionality
  • User code and smartphone app updates

User maintenance involves weekly testing using the test mode, cleaning PIR lenses quarterly, and monitoring system status indicators. Wireless systems require battery monitoring through smartphone apps or panel displays.

Professional service costs £80-£150 annually but prevents false alarms, ensures police response eligibility, and maintains manufacturer warranties. Some insurance policies require annual certification for claim validity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need planning permission for burglar alarm installation?

Planning permission is not required for burglar alarm installation in most UK residential properties. However, listed buildings or conservation areas may require consent for external sounders, and some housing associations have installation restrictions.

How long do burglar alarm batteries last?

Burglar alarm batteries typically last 3-5 years in control panels and 5-7 years in wireless sensors. Modern lithium batteries in Ajax systems provide up to 7 years life, while traditional sealed lead-acid batteries require replacement every 3-4 years.

Will my burglar alarm work during power cuts?

Professional burglar alarm systems include battery backup providing 12-24 hours operation during power failures. The system automatically switches to battery power and continues normal operation including monitoring centre communication.

Can I move my burglar alarm to a new house?

Wireless burglar alarm systems can be relocated to new properties with professional reconfiguration. Hardwired systems require complete reinstallation, making wireless systems more suitable for renters or frequent movers.

What happens if I accidentally trigger my burglar alarm?

Accidental alarm triggers should be cancelled immediately using your user code or smartphone app. Monitored systems will call within 60 seconds for verification - provide your codeword to prevent unnecessary police dispatch and potential false alarm charges.
